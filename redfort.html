Red Fort, India

Overview

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India, stands as a symbol of India's rich history and the power of the Mughal Empire. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, this massive red sandstone structure served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. With its intricate carvings, majestic gates, and sprawling courtyards, the fort continues to be a proud landmark where India celebrates its Independence Day annually.
